The **[Dharma](../../01_core_teachings/the_three_jewels/README.md#2-dharma--the-path-and-the-law-of-reality) Wheel** is the primary symbol of Buddhism because it encodes *how [awakening](../../10_concepts/README.md#3-enlightenment-bodhi-awakening) functions in the world*. It is not decorative; it is **operational doctrine**.

---

### 1. **Teaching in Motion**

When the Buddha gave his first sermon at Sarnath, tradition calls this **â€œturning the wheel of Dharma.â€**
This expresses a core insight:

* Truth is not static doctrine
* Dharma **moves**, **adapts**, and **acts**
* Teaching must be *applied*, not merely understood

A wheel only fulfills its purpose **while turning**. Likewise, Dharma exists only when practicedâ€”through conduct, [meditation](../../08_lineage/README.md), and insight.

### 4. **The Hub: Non-Dual Stability**

* Thoughts arise and pass (rim)
* Activity continues (spokes)
* Awareness remains still (hub)

This teaches a subtle but profound point:

> Liberation is not stopping movement â€” it is **not being thrown by it**.

---

### 5. **Why the Wheel, Not a Sword or Book**

Other traditions emphasize:

* Law (tablets)
* Salvation (cross)
* Power (sword)

Buddhism emphasizes **process**.

The wheel conveys:

* Continuity over revelation
* Training over belief
* Causality over authority

---

### 6. **Meditative Insight**

In practice, the Dharma Wheel reminds practitioners to ask:

* Is my life *turning* in alignment with insight?
* Are my actions reinforcing suffering or clarity?
* Is awareness centered, or dragged by the rim?

The wheel does not judge â€” it **reveals trajectory**.

---

### Core Teaching Summary

> The Dharma Wheel teaches that awakening is not a state to possess,
> but a **direction to sustain**.

When the wheel turns smoothly, suffering decreases.
When it warps, friction appears.
Practice is the art of **keeping the wheel true**.
