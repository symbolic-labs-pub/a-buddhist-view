The resolution used by major traditions:

* **Emptiness** describes the **absence of inherent existence**
* **Buddha-nature** describes the **absence of inherent obstruction**

> Because the mind is empty of fixed essence, it is capable of awakening.

Zen and Madhyamaka often phrase this carefully:

> Buddha-nature is **emptiness experienced without fear**.

### A concise doctrinal summary

* Buddha-nature affirms **universal awakenability**
* It does **not** reintroduce a self
* It harmonizes with emptiness when properly understood
* It prevents nihilism *and* arrogance
* It reframes practice as **revelation rather than construction**

> You do not become something new.
> You stop mistaking yourself for something smaller.
